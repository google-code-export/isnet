Mentor Dashboard Help

 

The Mentor dashboard is the default home page of the mentor. After successful login, the mentor is forwarded to the dashboard.

The Mentor dashboard is logically divided into three sections. The left most section,  the middle section and the right section.

 

1. Left section: The left section contains the navigation menu, story and viewpoint related sub sections. The success story relates to the experience of a Mentor who had success in following a particular action plan which resulted in the guest protégé wanting to learn more about Islam and Muslims and possibly resulted in the acceptance of Islam. Other mentors get to vote on ‘success’ stories to rank the best and top ten stories. You have to be a certified Mentor to post and vote for a story. The Viewpoint is the ‘view’ of the non-Muslim about Islam and Muslims which are posted by guests and protégés and other guests/protégés get to vote on the validity of the view point, resulting in the most voted to the least voted ranking. Each sub section is explained below:

 

 

1.1 Top ten stories section: This section contains the top ten success stories approved by the administrator of the site. You have to be a certified mentor to vote for a story. You become certified by completing the Da’wah training, paying the $35 certification fee and passing the interview with a certified mentor. After you submit a story, it is then approved by the admin. After the admin confirms the story, it is available on the dashboard and ready for voting.

1.2 Winning story section: Every week there is a story competition, submitted by mentors. The most voted story is selected and displayed in this section.

1.3 Winning viewpoint section: Every week there is a viewpoint competition, submitted by protégés or guests. The most voted viewpoint is selected and displayed in this section.

1.4 Success stories: The section all the stories submitted by all mentors. Only five success story headlines are displayed at a time. Clicking on each headline will display the details of that story in a popup window.

1.5 Browse more stories (Story Voting): After clicking the browse more stories user is forwarded to another page contains all the stories with the ability to vote each stories. Each user can vote for only one story. You have to be a certified mentor to vote on a story.

 

 

 

2. Middle section: The middle section contains mentor requests, protégé requests and mentor reporting sub section. Each sub section is explained below:

2.1 New protégé section: This section contains the request coming from a prospective protégé to the mentor. You have the option to either accept or decline/reject the request. You have the option to add a note during accepting or rejecting the request. If you accept the request as the mentor then the potential protégé or guest will become your protégé. At this point you have the obligation of looking after, teaching about Islam and Muslims to and guiding your protégé.

2.2 New mentor request: This section contains all the mentor requests coming from other mentors. If you accept this mentor request, you will be the lead mentor of the mentor who sends the request. Your obligations of being a lead mentor are to help the other mentor become successful in giving da’wah.

2.3 Pending activities: This section contains activity requests coming from a mentor or guest/ protégé. The activity request may be a personal meeting request, visit request etc.

2.4 Mentor reporting section: The section contains all the mentors or protégés who are under your mentorship.  If you are a lead mentor then there will be a list of mentors and their protégés listed in this section. The mentor also has to option to monitor the feedback and activities of each mentor or protégé under him/her.

 

3. Right section: The right section contains the mentor certification expiry date, mentor to do list, what's new section and the important link section.

3.1 Expiry date section: This section contains the mentor certification expiry date.

3.2 Things To do list: This section contains the list of all tasks which need to be performed by the mentor. For instance, the mentor first has to finish the da’wah training and tutorial, successfully complete the tests, pay the annual certification fee, etc.

3.3 What’s new section: This section contains recent news posted by the myc2i site admin.

3.4 Important links section: The section contains the list of important web sites related to Islam
